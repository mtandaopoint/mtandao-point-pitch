The Problem

Over 20 million Tanzanians are still offline due to unaffordable internet costs and poor infrastructure, particularly in rural areas. This digital exclusion limits access to essential services such as education, healthcare, finance, and civic participation.

Key challenges include:

  • Unreliable Connectivity: High costs and limited internet infrastructure hinder digital access in rural communities.
  • Lack of Digital Identity: More than 15 million Tanzanians lack a formal digital identity, making it difficult to access essential services such as banking and healthcare.
  • Financial Exclusion: Many rural communities have limited access to mobile banking and mobile money services, further deepening economic disparities.

Our Solution

MTANDAO POINT addresses the key challenges of digital exclusion, lack of digital identity, and financial inaccessibility in rural Tanzania by providing an integrated, community-driven platform.

Our solution combines:

  • Affordable Connectivity: Solar-powered mesh Wi-Fi networks to deliver internet access even in remote villages.
  • Blockchain-Based Digital Identity: Secure, decentralized digital identities using Hyperledger technology to give individuals control over their personal data and improve access to services.
  • Mobile Money Integration: Collaboration with mobile money providers (M-Pesa, Airtel Money, NMB) to offer financial services, including microloans, mobile payments, and digital wallets.
  • Community-Driven Governance: Local councils and community members will manage and maintain the infrastructure, ensuring sustainability and ownership.

How It Works

MTANDAO POINT leverages local infrastructure, solar energy, and inclusive governance to deliver sustainable connectivity and digital empowerment in rural Tanzania.

  • 1. Connectivity: We partner with ISPs to purchase wholesale bandwidth and distribute it through solar-powered mesh networks using TP-Link and LibreRouter hardware.
  • 2. Access: Users connect via affordable daily plans (TZS 1,000/day), with subsidized options for schools, farmers, and women’s groups.
  • 3. Digital Identity: Residents are issued blockchain-based digital IDs (Hyperledger) for secure access to services and credit.
  • 4. Financial Inclusion: Integration with mobile wallets (M-Pesa, Airtel Money, NMB) allows for seamless digital transactions.
  • 5. Local Ownership: Communities manage and maintain networks, share revenues, and reinvest in local development (education, health, microloans).

This community-centric model ensures affordability, scalability, and resilience—empowering rural populations from the ground up.

Risks & Mitigation

We have identified key risks and devised strategies to ensure the success of MTANDAO POINT:

  • Power Outages: Mitigation: Solar-powered system with battery backups and mesh routing for reliability.
  • ISP Delays: Mitigation: Established MoUs with multiple ISPs, regulatory incentives, and alternative partnerships to ensure bandwidth supply.
  • Community Trust: Mitigation: Engage local leaders, peer educators, and a demo-first approach to build confidence in the solution.
  • Funding Constraints: Mitigation: Diversified funding streams from users, grants, and social investors, along with targeted fundraising campaigns.
  • Privacy & Data Security: Mitigation: Implement a blockchain-based system for data protection and local data governance to safeguard user privacy.
